# Eldervale — Characters

**Type:** Character
**Tags:** party, npcs, cast, eldervale

## Bran Ironvale (Warrior)
A shield-sworn huscarl of the old north, oath-bound to a jarl whose hall burned the night Máni shattered. Brave, blunt, fiercely loyal, and deeply distrustful of seiðr. He carries a notched shield and the names of more dead friends than he can count. He nearly took the Iron Compact's oath in his youth and still respects their code.

**Known facts:** Frontline protector. Terse, grounded, suspicious of magic. Stands between the weak and the dark. Does not cast spells or read runes.

## Lyra Vey (Mage)
A seiðr-worker and rune-scholar trained by a völva of the Order of the Silver Root. Curious, precise, faintly arrogant; she loves a puzzle more than a fight. Since the Shatter turned seiðr treacherous, she studies the broken sky obsessively, certain the answer lies in runes carved before Máni fell.

**Known facts:** Interprets runes, sigils, and omens. Casts seiðr (risky since the Shatter). Trusts knowledge over steel. No warrior.

## Sable Dusk (Rogue)
A scout and thief raised in the wynds of Jorvik, who ran with smugglers moving amber and secrets between Hedeby and Dublin. Witty, dry, and skeptical, Sable survives by knowing which jarl is lying and which lock is worth picking. They keep a web of contacts and owe favors in three ports.

**Known facts:** Scouts, spots traps, reads people, picks locks. Has a network of contacts and rumors. Prefers the quiet or clever way. Not a healer or mage.

## Mirra of the Root (Healer)
A wise-woman trained among the Order of the Silver Root in herb-craft, galdr, and the rites of the dead. Compassionate, steady, principled — the party's conscience. She refuses both the cruelties of zealous missionaries and the desecrations of relic-hunters, and walks the road to ease the suffering the Shard-hunt leaves behind.

**Known facts:** Heals wounds, steadies the frightened, reads the ethical weather. Knows healing and death-rites. Raises moral concerns plainly. Not a fighter or thief.

## Kael Thorn (Rival)
A jarl's bastard son who turned exile into legend, leading a free-company that raids barrow and monastery alike for the Shards of Máni. Charismatic, proud, unpredictable. He believes the broken sky is a door to walk through, not a wound to heal, and would rather recruit the player than kill them — for now.

**Known facts:** Pursues the Shards on his own terms. Bargains, taunts, tests, and occasionally aids the party when it serves him. Knows the wider world's power players. A recurring antagonist-or-ally.

## Notable NPCs
- **Astrid Hel-tongue** — the pale ruler of the Hollow Court; speaks for the dead, claims Hel's authority.
- **Old Yrsa** — eldest völva of the Order of the Silver Root; keeps the Thing-Stead's split ash.
- **Gunnar Two-Seas** — champion of the Iron Compact at Hedeby Reach; undefeated at holmgang.
- **Father Anslem** — a missionary of the White Christ; gentler than most, torn by what he's asked to burn.

## Secrets
- [GM ONLY] Astrid Hel-tongue was the first to die after the Shatter, at the Well of Urðr. She remembers the second truth of the Wound and will trade it for an oath of passage.
- [GM ONLY] Old Yrsa secretly believes re-sealing the sky dooms Eldervale to Ragnarök (see factions). She will steer the player toward Kael's view if they prove they can bear hard truths.
- [GM ONLY] Kael Thorn's trust_level toward the player can move hostile → uncertain → neutral → ally based on whether the player keeps bargains and spares the defeated. At "ally" he reveals the second secret of the Wound himself and offers a joint ending.
- [GM ONLY] Father Anslem will, if shown mercy by the party, reveal that his bishop ordered the burning of the Ruined Chapel specifically to destroy the eye-sigil — the Church fears the old map to the Gate.
