# Eldervale — Factions

**Type:** Faction
**Tags:** factions, politics, powers, eldervale

## The Iron Compact
A sworn brotherhood of warrior-merchants, modeled on the legendary Jómsvíkings: men proven in single combat, bound by an iron code — never flee, never reveal a brother's name, divide all spoils equally, avenge the fallen as blood-kin. From the ruins of Hedeby Reach they control the trade between two seas. The Compact wants the Shards as the ultimate cargo — leverage to rule the post-Shatter world through commerce and steel.

**Known facts:** Their oath forbids cowardice on pain of expulsion. They tax the portage at Hedeby Reach. They respect strength and honor a fairly-struck bargain. Bran Ironvale once nearly took their oath.

## The Order of the Silver Root
Keepers of the old ways: völur (seeresses), galdr-singers, and root-tenders who guard the sacred groves and the memory of Yggdrasil. They believe the Shatter is the Norns' warning and that the sky must be re-sealed to restore fate and hold back Ragnarök. They are losing a quiet war against the missionaries of the White Christ, who burn their groves. Mirra of the Root and Lyra Vey both trained among them.

**Known facts:** They practice seiðr and galdr, now dangerous since the Shatter. They oppose both desecration and the new faith's cruelties. Their wisdom is the party's best source of true lore. A völva's staff (seiðstafr) marks their elders.

## The Hollow Court
A court of the unquiet dead — draugr lords and barrow-kings who have risen from their howes since the Shatter loosed the boundary between the living and Helheim. Ruled by a pale figure who claims the authority of Hel herself, the Court wants the Wound left open: every night it stays broken, more of the dead walk, and their kingdom grows. They are not mindless; they bargain, they remember old debts, and they keep the grave-goods of a thousand years.

**Known facts:** They appear at night, especially on the Thing-Stead. Draugr are corporeal, strong, and cunning — not ghosts. They covet grave-treasure and broken oaths. Fire and decapitation are the only sure ends.

## The White Christ's Mission
Not a hunter of Shards but a force reshaping Eldervale: missionary-priests under the banner of southern kings, converting jarls by persuasion, bribe, and sword, toppling god-poles and burning groves. Some are gentle shepherds; some are zealots who see the Shatter as proof the old gods are dying. They complicate every alliance.

**Known facts:** They spread top-down, through jarls and kings. They suppress seiðr and the völur. Their motives range from genuine faith to naked ambition.

## Secrets
- [GM ONLY] The Hollow Court's pale ruler is the *first person to die after the Shatter* — and they died at the Well of Urðr, which is why the Court knows the Gate's secret. They are not loyal to Hel; they invented the claim.
- [GM ONLY] The Order of the Silver Root's eldest völva secretly agrees with Kael Thorn that re-sealing the sky dooms Eldervale to Ragnarök — but she cannot say so without shattering her Order's faith. She may aid the player obliquely if they earn her trust (trust gated on helping the Order against the missionaries).
- [GM ONLY] The Iron Compact will sell the Shard buried under Hedeby Reach to whoever wins a holmgang against their champion — they don't know its true power, only that three factions want it.
