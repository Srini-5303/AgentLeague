# Eldervale — Locations

**Type:** Location
**Tags:** places, locations, map, eldervale

## The Ruined Chapel
A raided hof-turned-mission on a windswept headland — once a temple to the old gods, then seized and re-consecrated to the White Christ, then burned in the chaos after the Shatter. Now it is a roofless shell of salt-bleached timber and toppled god-poles, where both faiths' dead lie unburied. An **eye-sigil** is carved above the broken altar, older than either faith. This is where the party's hunt for the Shards begins; the sigil is the first key to the Moonlit Gate.

**Known facts:** Travelers avoid it after dark. A cold wind threads the nave even in still weather. The carvings beneath the ash are pre-Shatter.

## Jorvik-on-the-Ouse
A teeming Norse-held trade city under Danelaw law, where Danish, Saxon, and Irish merchants haggle side by side in narrow wynds thick with the smell of tanneries and tar. Amber, hacksilver, furs, and slaves change hands; so do rumors. Sable Dusk knows its rooftops and its smugglers. The city is neutral ground — for now — but every faction keeps agents here.

**Known facts:** Ruled in name by a jarl who answers to no king. Silver is weighed on the spot; cheats lose fingers. The best information is sold in the dockside ale-halls.

## Hedeby Reach
The charred remains of the great market-town of Hedeby, burned four winters past at the narrowest waist of the land — gateway between the North Sea and the Baltic. Refugees and raiders now squat in its ruins. The Iron Compact has made the Reach its base, controlling the overland portage and taxing all who cross.

**Known facts:** Whoever holds the Reach holds trade between two seas. The Iron Compact's sworn brothers patrol it. Glass and amber beadwork still surfaces in the ash.

## The Thing-Stead of Holmgang Isle
A flat, sacred island where the free folk gather to make law beneath standing stones and a lightning-split ash. Weapons are oath-bound to silence on the island; disputes are settled by formal duel (holmgang) at its edge. Since the Shatter the assembly has fractured, and the island has become a place where the three factions negotiate — and scheme.

**Known facts:** Violence on the Thing-Stead breaks the deepest oath in Eldervale. The split ash is said to be a sapling of Yggdrasil. The Hollow Court has begun appearing at night.

## The Moonlit Gate at the Well of Urðr
Beneath a glacier at the roof of the world, three roots of a vast ash plunge into a black mirror-pool: the **Well of Urðr**, where the Norns water the World Tree and weave fate. A great rune-graven arch — the **Moonlit Gate** — stands over the water. To open it, a seeker must speak **three conflicting truths** into the well. This is the heart of the campaign: where the Shards must be brought, and where the sky will be sealed or torn.

**Known facts:** The pool never freezes. The Norns are said to still keep the Well, though no living soul has seen them since the Shatter. The Gate's runes shift when watched.

## The Memory Vault
Reached only through the open Moonlit Gate: a frozen splinter of the world *as it was before the moon broke* — a pre-Shatter kingdom held motionless in pale moonlight, its people stopped mid-breath. Here the last whole Shard of Máni waits. To take it, the party must move through a past that does not want to be remembered.

**Known facts:** Time runs strangely within. The air tastes of the night the moon was whole. The figures within are not statues.

## Secrets
- [GM ONLY] The Ruined Chapel's eye-sigil is a map of the Well of Urðr's rune-sequence; rubbing it with moon-ash (from the Wound) reveals the three truths the Gate demands. Lyra Vey can decipher this with an Arcana check at DC 15.
- [GM ONLY] Hedeby Reach hides a buried Shard in the ash of the old mint, beneath the Iron Compact's feet — they do not know they are sitting on it.
- [GM ONLY] The figures in the Memory Vault are the pre-Shatter dead, and one of them wears Kael Thorn's face. The Memory Vault is where Kael learned the second secret of the Wound. It will offer the player the same knowledge — at a price.
- [GM ONLY] Speaking three truths that are all *comforting lies* opens the Gate but tears the Wound wider. The three truths must each cost the speaker something real.
