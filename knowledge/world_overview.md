# Eldervale — World Overview

**Type:** World
**Tags:** setting, eldervale, mani, shatter, ragnarok, norse

## The Realm
Eldervale is a cold, sea-riven land of fjords, fells, and longhouse-halls, woven from the late Viking Age (the era between the burning of Lindisfarne and the fall of Harald Hardrada). Jarls rule from timbered halls; free karls farm and bear arms; thralls labor and dream of freedom. Once a year the free folk gather at a Thing to make law, settle blood-feuds, and trade. Silver is weighed, not counted, and a man's word is worth his hoard.

Three faiths grind against one another: the old gods of Asgard and Vanaheim, the fate-craft of seiðr, and the rising White Christ carried north by missionaries who burn the sacred groves. Into this Máni fell.

## The Shatter
Three winters ago, **Máni — the moon — broke apart in the night sky.** For twenty-three days a fierce "guest star" burned so bright it cast shadows at noon; then it faded to a smear of pale fire that lingers still, called the **Wound** by those who saw it. (In the dry tongue of distant astronomers this was a star's death; in Eldervale it is the beginning of the end.)

Since the Shatter, the old certainties have failed. **Seiðr has turned wild** — spells bite the hand that casts them. **The dead no longer keep to their howes**; draugr walk the barrow-roads. The Norns, it is whispered, have lost the thread of fate, and Ragnarök — long prophesied for when the sky-wolves Sköll and Hati at last devour sun and moon — now feels not like a tale but a date.

## The Race for the Shards
Máni did not vanish. It fell to earth as the **Shards of Máni** — fragments of moon-stone, each humming with the power that once held the night together. Gathered at the **Well of Urðr**, where the Norns water the World Tree, the Shards might re-seal the broken sky. Or, spoken with the wrong truths, they might tear the Wound wide and let what waits behind it through.

Five powers hunt the Shards. The player's party must reach the Well first — before the rival Kael Thorn, before the dead, before the sky finishes breaking.

## Tone
Dark, curious, and morally complex. There are no clean victories in Eldervale, only choices and their weight. The gods are real but distant; the dead remember; and every saga is told by someone who survived to tell it.

## Secrets
- [GM ONLY] The Wound is not Máni's death but a *door*. Behind it waits not Fenrir alone but the pre-Shatter age itself, frozen and watching — the world as it was before the moon broke. The Memory Vault (see locations) is a splinter of that frozen past.
- [GM ONLY] The Norns did not *lose* the thread of fate. They *cut* it — deliberately — to give mortals a single generation of true free will before Ragnarök. The Shatter is that cut made visible. Re-sealing the sky restores fate and dooms Eldervale to the prophesied end; leaving it broken keeps the future unwritten but lets the dead and the giants spill through. There is no painless ending.
- [GM ONLY] Kael Thorn knows the second secret. The player does not. This is why he wants the Wound left open.
